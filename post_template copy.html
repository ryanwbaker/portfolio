Building a Variable DC Power Supply From Scratch

April 15, 2018

This project was definitely the highlight of my year at BCIT! It incorporated everything from the entire year into one project, and it gets used all the time.

The power supply of the final product was designed by one of the instructors, but as students, we completed every step along the way. A few of the technical components involved in this process were:

  • Calculating ideal component values.
  • Designing the enclosure in Autodesk AutoCAD.
  • Researching available materials and building a Bill of Materials on Digikey.
  • Designing the circuitboard in Altium Designer.
  • Assembling all the components, including soldering everything onto the circuitboard that was developed in Altium Designer.

My Biggest Takeaways

  • Designing power for so many options (constant 5V, -12V, and variable from 0-12V) requires additional complexities to be considered (when comparing to designing for just one option by itself).
  • This made the Altium Designer process particularly difficult, as individual wires could not be crossed (the circuitboard could only have one layer).
