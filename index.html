About Me

Ever since I was young, I've always had a fascination with how things worked. In fact, I'd say that my curiosity is my biggest strength. In my younger years, I would always take things apart and put them back together again, just for fun. Fast forward to high school, I played many sports, and suffered some injuries that required several surgeries. The surgeries (like most things) sparked my curiosity, but this time with how the human body worked, which led me into studying kinesiology right out of high school. It's funny, looking back, my parents really encouraged me to go into engineering school, but my curiosity got the better of me and I was set on learning about the human body. After completing kinesiology and working as a personal trainer for a few years, while I valued the knowledge I gained in kinesiology, I found that I was just not passionate about working in that industry (training unmotivated people, the rise of misinformation on the internet, etc.). I thought to myself "What is something that is always changing, but also valuable in any industry? Business." This led me to pursuing a Master's Degree in Business Administration.

While the business knowledge I gained was also incredibly valuable, I once again found myself wanting more. My business education has given me an entrepreneurial mindset that loves to think about innovation and solving problems...but there was one problem. I didn't know how to build the solutions I dreamed up.

Finally, at 30 years old, I decided to pursue engineering school. I completed a year of Electrical Engineering Technology at BCIT, but due to a family-related difficulty, I was forced to take a year off. When the year was over, I was not allowed back into the program. This turned to be a blessing in disguise, as I decided to apply for engineering school at the University of Calgary, and got accepted!

Having been an engineering student for awhile now, I can say with absolute confidence that engineering is the right profession for me. Though, academically, these past few years have been the most difficult, they have also been the most enjoyable. It's true what they say about finding your passion, "Do what you love and you'll never work a day in your life". I understand that statement on an intimate level since I began studying engineering. Some of the biggest takeaways I've had from this academic adventure are:

  • Finish what you start,
  • Don't be afraid of learning, and
  • Sometimes, your parents really do know best!
